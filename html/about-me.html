About Me

My name is William Collins and I grew up in south central Iowa. I graduated from Centerville High School and am currently enrolled in the Computer Software Development program offered at Indian Hills Community College following an emphasis in mainframe development. I began tinkering with computers as a kid, working with mods for video games I played at the time, such as Minecraft and Call of Duty: World at War. When I was a teenager, I finally got myself a computer capable of gaming and modding video games, which introduced me to the idea of programming. Through modding/creating video games, I learned that coding was my passion and a path I wish to pursue in my life to make a career. Even though I had many passions in my life I could morph into a career, such as music, I decided that software development and programming fit my skillset and my ambitions perfectly. Throughout the end of middle school and leading up into and through high school, I practiced coding in my freetime, eventually taking Computer Science Principles, a class offered at my school for the first time during my sophomore year of high school.
